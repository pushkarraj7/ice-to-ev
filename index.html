ICE to EV Conversion

With rising fuel costs and mounting environmental concerns, the transition from Internal Combustion Engine (ICE) vehicles to Electric Vehicles (EVs) is accelerating.

Across the globe, ICE-powered vehicles—buses, taxis, delivery vans, and personal cars—are making way for cleaner, smarter electric mobility.

Policy Supports for Powering the EV Movement

Delhi’s EV Policy 2.0 Highlights

  • ICE Three-Wheelers: No new registration after August 2025; existing CNG permits converted to EV-only. Read More
  • ICE Two-Wheelers: New registrations banned from August 2026; strong incentives and subsidies continue. Read More
  • ICE Cars: Limit to two ICE cars per household—third must be electric. Read More

Goal: 95% of new registrations electric by 2027, rising to 98% by 2030. Read More

Infrastructure: Fast-charging every 5 km, battery swapping hubs, and ~20,000 EV jobs. Read More

Nationwide & Global

  • India (FAME II): Subsidies for retrofitted EVs, public transport electrification, and infrastructure development.
  • Global: Countries like the UK, Germany, and US offer tax credits, rebates, and low-emission zones to accelerate EV adoption and ICE phase-outs.

With strong policy backing, converting to EV isn’t just smart—it’s strategic.
